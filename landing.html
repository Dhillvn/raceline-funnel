Questions, answered

The questions owners actually ask.

No script and no pressure — just how a considered sale works, and exactly what happens to your car and your details.

Anything we've not covered here, just ask when we speak. There's never any obligation.

The indicative range you see is given in good faith from what you've told us. The appraisal is there to confirm it — not to find reasons to chip you. If the car is as described, the figure holds. If something genuinely differs, we'll show you exactly why, openly and with no theatrics.

It's a considered guide — not an offer, and not a deadline. There's nothing to accept and no clock running. We give a range rather than a single tempting number precisely so we're not over-promising to win the enquiry. It's a figure we can stand behind in person.

None. There's no charge for a valuation and no commitment to sell — no agreement to sign and nothing to pay. If the figure isn't right for you, that's absolutely fine: you walk away with no obligation.

Once the appraisal and paperwork are complete, payment is made by secure bank transfer the same day — funds typically clear within hours. Prefer not to travel? Nationwide collection can usually be arranged within 24 hours.

Your details stay with Raceline Specialist Cars and the buying team alone. We never sell or pass them on, and they're used only to prepare your valuation and arrange the appraisal. If you'd rather your sale stayed quiet, it can — like the best specialists, we're happy to handle a car discreetly and, where appropriate, sell it on without ever advertising it publicly.

Privately, you're exposing your car — and your time — to strangers, drawn-out viewings and payment risk, often for weeks. At auction you pay fees and inherit the result on the day. Selling to Raceline Specialist Cars removes that: one professional buyer, a straightforward process, and payment by bank transfer once we've agreed. The convenience is the point.
